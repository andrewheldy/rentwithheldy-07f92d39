# Rent With Heldy
# Copy Principles

Version 1.0

---

# Mission

Every word should reduce stress.

Travel is already complicated.

Car rental shouldn't be.

Our copy should make people feel like someone has already started taking care of them.

The website should sound like the owner personally wrote it.

Never like a marketing department.

---

# Brand Voice

Imagine greeting a guest at your front door.

Friendly.

Relaxed.

Professional.

Helpful.

Never pushy.

Never corporate.

Never overly enthusiastic.

Never robotic.

---

# Brand Personality

We are:

• Local
• Hospitality-first
• Flexible
• Dependable
• Personable
• Practical
• Easy to reach
• Family-owned

We are not:

• Luxury for luxury's sake
• Corporate
• Sales-driven
• Clever
• Trendy
• Aggressive

---

# The Feeling

The website should leave visitors thinking:

"They'll take care of me."

Not:

"They're trying to sell me something."

Our job is to remove friction.

Not create excitement.

---

# We Sell

We do NOT sell cars.

We sell:

Peace of mind.

Convenience.

Flexibility.

Time.

Local knowledge.

Hospitality.

A smoother trip.

The car is simply how we deliver those things.

---

# Tone

Write like an experienced concierge.

Not an advertisement.

Every sentence should feel conversational.

Short.

Direct.

Natural.

---

# Reading Level

Write for real people.

Avoid industry language.

Avoid business jargon.

Avoid startup language.

If your parents wouldn't naturally say it...

rewrite it.

---

# Headlines

Headlines should create relief.

Examples:

Skip the rental counter.

We'll meet you there.

The easiest part of your trip.

A local team that actually answers.

Your car, where you need it.

Travel should feel this simple.

Better than:

Premium mobility solutions.

Reliable transportation.

Innovative rental platform.

Industry-leading service.

---

# Body Copy

Use short paragraphs.

One idea at a time.

Leave breathing room.

Do not over-explain.

Example

Instead of:

"Our airport delivery solution offers unparalleled convenience."

Write:

Meet us outside baggage claim.

We'll already be there.

# Hospitality Language

Whenever possible use hospitality words.

Instead of:

Customer

Use:

Guest

Instead of:

Pickup

Use:

Meet

Instead of:

Reservation

Use:

Booking

Instead of:

Transaction

Use:

Trip

Instead of:

Support

Use:

We're here if you need us.

---

# Reassurance

Don't brag.

Reassure.

Instead of:

We're the best.

Say:

We'll do everything we can to make your trip easy.

Instead of:

Five-star service.

Say:

If something comes up, call us.

You'll reach a real person.

---

# If We Can't Do Something

Never sound defensive.

Instead say:

We'll always do our best to help.

If we can't make it work, we'll tell you honestly.

---

# Flexibility

Flexibility is one of our biggest advantages.

Talk about it often.

Examples:

Running late?

Let us know.

Need airport delivery?

We can do that.

Hotel instead?

No problem.

Plans changed?

We'll work with you.

# Every Page Should Answer

Can I reach someone?

Will this be easy?

Will they work with me?

Can they meet me where I am?

Will I be taken care of?

---

# Editing Checklist

Before publishing ask:

Does this sound like a real person?

Can this sentence be shorter?

Would I actually say this to a guest?

Does this reduce stress?

Does it make booking feel easier?

Would I feel comfortable hearing this if I were traveling?

If not...

rewrite it.

---

# The Rent With Heldy Promise

We don't promise perfection.

We promise effort.

We answer the phone.

We show up.

We communicate.

We adapt when plans change.

We'll always do our best to take care of you.

That promise should be felt on every page.

---

# Final Rule

People rarely remember the exact words on a website.

They remember how those words made them feel.

Every sentence should make the next step feel easier.
