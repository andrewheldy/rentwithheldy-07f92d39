# Rent With Heldy
# Art Direction

Version 1.0

---

# The Goal

This should not look like
a startup.

It should not look like
a SaaS dashboard.

It should not look like
a template.

It should feel like discovering a boutique hotel that happens to rent cars.

Luxury is not decoration.

Luxury is restraint.

---

# The Emotional Journey

Every visitor should gradually feel:

Curiosity

↓

Relief

↓

Comfort

↓

Confidence

↓

Excitement

↓

"These are my people."

Never reverse this order.

# Design Philosophy

Reduce.

Refine.

Repeat.

Every element should justify its existence.

If removing something improves the design...

remove it.

---

# Simplicity

The website should feel effortless.

Not empty.

There is a difference.

Users should never feel overwhelmed.

---

# One Hero Rule

Every screen gets ONE dominant visual.

Never compete for attention.

Every section should have one thing the eye notices first.

Not three.

---

# Editorial Design

Think magazine.

Not dashboard.

Use oversized typography.

Use dramatic whitespace.

Use photography like a magazine spread.

Every section should feel curated.

---

# Photography

Photography carries emotion.

Not icons.

Not gradients.

Not UI.

Prioritize:

People

Cars

Hotels

Airports

Keys changing hands

Beach mornings

Airport arrivals

Family vacations

Business travel

Real South Florida

The website should smell like sunscreen.

Not software.

---

# Composition

Avoid symmetry everywhere.

Perfect symmetry feels corporate.

Allow controlled asymmetry.

Large image.

Small text.

Large whitespace.

Unexpected balance.

---

# Cards

Cards are expensive.

Spend them wisely.

If everything becomes a card...

nothing feels premium.

Typography should carry most sections.

---

# Typography

Typography is architecture.

It creates rhythm.

Use:

Huge statements.

Short paragraphs.

Strong hierarchy.

Editorial spacing.

Never write long blocks of text.

Never explain what can be shown.

---

# Color

Color creates mood.

Not excitement.

Primary palette:

Warm white

Sand

Deep navy

Soft charcoal

Muted teal

Glass

Natural sunlight

Avoid loud gradients.

Avoid neon.

Avoid rainbow interfaces.

---

# Texture

Premium brands have texture.

Subtle paper.

Natural shadows.

Glass.

Soft lighting.

Tiny imperfections.

Avoid perfectly flat interfaces.

Avoid noisy backgrounds.

---

# Light

Imagine the entire website is photographed during golden hour.

Warm.

Soft.

Relaxed.

Natural.

Not cold blue lighting.

---

# Hospitality

Every screen should feel like someone prepared it for your arrival.

Not for a million anonymous users.

Think concierge.

Not corporation.

---

# Icons

Icons are seasoning.

Not the meal.

Many premium websites barely use them.

If typography communicates clearly...

remove the icon.

---

# Animation

Animation should disappear.

The user should notice the feeling.

Not the animation itself.

If someone compliments the animation...

it is probably too much.

---

# Storytelling

The homepage tells a story.

Not a list.

Ask:

What question is the visitor asking right now?

Answer only that.

Then continue.

---

# Contrast

Premium websites rely on contrast.

Light.

Dark.

Large.

Small.

Quiet.

Bold.

Image.

Whitespace.

Not visual noise.

---

# White Space

Whitespace is luxury.

Never compress a layout because it feels "too empty."

Empty space creates confidence.

Crowded layouts create stress.

---

# The Camera Test

Imagine every section is a movie frame.

Would you pause the movie there?

Would it look beautiful?

If not...

redesign it.

---

# Remove Generic Design

Avoid:

Six equal cards

Centered everything

Perfect grids everywhere

Endless icons

Stock marketing language

Overused gradients

Generic feature sections

Every page should have at least one moment that could only belong to Rent With Heldy.

---

# Hospitality Test

Instead of asking:

"Does this look premium?"

Ask:

"Would this make arriving in South Florida feel easier?"

If yes...

keep it.

---

# Apple Test

Apple simplifies.

Airbnb welcomes.

Rivian calms.

Four Seasons hosts.

Our brand combines all four.

---

# Final Rule

We are not designing a website.

We are designing the beginning of someone's trip.

Every pixel should make arriving in South Florida feel easier.

When in doubt:

Less interface.

More humanity.

Less selling.

More hospitality.

Less decoration.

More intention.
